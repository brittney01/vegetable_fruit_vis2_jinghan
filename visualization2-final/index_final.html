🥪 DIET IN WORLD 🥗

Food is both key to nurturing human healthand a major global challenge. Dietary habits vary geographically and culturally and have a profound impact on health, and poor diets are strongly associated with chronic diseases. it's crucial to comprehend regional dietary patterns, enhance diet quality, support sustainable food production, and foster global cooperation.

To disseminate the importance of diet, this tool provides a series of visualisations that depict diet copmposition across the world, the impact of wealth on diet and a dietary implications on health.

What is the recommended diet?

In 2019, the EAT-Lancet commission on healthy diets released a report describing the composition of a scientifically supported healthy 2500 calorie adult diet. Their target diet was divided into plant protein, animal protein, carbohydrates as well as fats and sugars. Although the ideal diet varies from person to person, they aim to quantify a universal healthy diet that is also environmentally sustainable.

Diet and Its Impact on Global Health

Diet plays a key role in global health and has a profound impact on the development of obesity and chronic diseases. Excessive intake of foods high in calories, fat and sugar is one of the major contributing factors to obesity, which is strongly associated with a wide range of chronic diseases. These include cardiovascular disease, diabetes, hypertension, certain cancers, and osteoporosis.

Thealth agencies and governments around the world have emphasized the importance of healthy eating by encouraging people to reduce their intake of foods high in salt, fat and sugar, increase consumption of vegetables, fruits and whole grains, and maintain a moderate body weight. These measures have helped to reduce obesity rates, improve global health and reduce the risk of hronic diseasec.

Obesity is considered a rapidly rising pandemic, where alarmingly 1.9 billion adults (39%) worldwide were considered overweight with 650 million (13%) of those considered obese in 2016. A body mass index (BMI) over 25 is considered overweight, and a BMI over 30 is considered obese.

The choropleth map below demonstrates the distribution of obesity and overweightness, across the world, over time. At the moment, more people are obese than underweight in every region except sub-Saharan Africa and Asia. Interestingly, the regions with the highest levels of obesity are located in small Pacific Island States due to a high amount of imported foods high in salt and fat.

Diseases Caused by Poor Diet

It is easy to see that inappropriate diets are most commonly associated with the incidence of heart disease, due to the close relationship between diet and blood pressure, ionic balance in the body and circulatory health. Processed meats and red meats, for example, are high in fat, and their overconsumption can lead to a buildup of fat in the arteries and veins, which increases the risk of heart disease and stroke.

In addition, diet is strongly associated with the incidence of cancer, diabetes and stroke. The risk of cancer is influenced by diets high in sugar, fat and fiber, particularly colon, breast and stomach cancers. The risk of diabetes is influenced by diets high in sugar and carbohydrates, which may lead to weight gain and insulin resistance. In addition, unhealthy eating habits can contribute to risk factors for stroke, as high blood pressure and weight gain are among the major risk factors for stroke.
